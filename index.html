ABOUT ME

A Student of
Climate and Complexity

With a foundation in physics and subsequent training in ocean and atmospheric sciences, my work is shaped by a curiosity about how complex systems work. I recently completed my PhD at the Centre for Atmospheric Sciences, IIT Delhi, where I studied changes in winds and precipitation over South Asia using observations, large-ensemble simulations, and process-based diagnostics.

My research is motivated by a simple but persistent question: how do regional climate systems respond to global warming in the presence of competing local forcings? Addressing this has led me to work across detection and attribution, numerical experimentation, and uncertainty analysis to disentangle forced signals from internal variability.

I am particularly interested in advancing credible regional climate information by strengthening the connection between models, physical interpretation, and real-world decision contexts.

Beyond research, I value the subtle rhythms of life — thoughtful conversations, quiet perseverance, and the belief that empathy and humility are essential companions in science as in life. If you wish to know the person behind the work, you may explore Stories.

RESEARCH FOCUS

Areas of Expertise

My research sits at the intersection of climate physics, data science, and societal impact, aiming to bridge the gap between scientific understanding and actionable insights.

Climate Dynamics

Diagnosing long-term changes in subtropical westerlies and monsoon circulation using reanalysis datasets and CESM large ensembles. Quantifying the competing roles of greenhouse gas–driven warming and regionally heterogeneous forcings (aerosols and land-use change) in shaping spatially asymmetric wind responses over South Asia.

Regional ForcingMonsoon WindsClimate Change

Detection and Attribution

Separating externally forced signals from internal variability using CESM LENS, CAM6 perturbed-parameter ensembles, and CMIP/DAMIP archives. Evaluating robustness of historical trends through multi-member statistics and single-forcing experiments, with emphasis on uncertainty in regional precipitation projections.

AttributionSingle-ForcingInternal Variability

Uncertainty and Process Diagnosis

Investigating the relationship between SST warming and regional precipitation response through targeted numerical experiments (CESM CAM5/CAM6). Identifying structural and parametric sensitivities that govern circulation–precipitation coupling, particularly along convective margins and secondary rainfall zones.

SST CouplingSensitivity ExperimentsHydrological Response

Climate Data Analysis

Experienced in working with large volumes of observational and model datasets, including large ensemble simulations (CESM LENS, CAM6 PPE), single-forcing large ensembles, and multi-model archives (CMIP/DAMIP). Proficient in systematic model evaluation, detection and attribution of climate change, sensitivity experiments, and physically consistent diagnostic frameworks for process-based understanding of regional circulation and precipitation changes.

Numerical Experiments & Model Diagnostics

Developed and customized modeling workflows, including contributions to the SARCI framework for regionally tailored climate simulations. Designed and analyzed targeted numerical experiments to isolate the impacts of external forcings and to diagnose structural and parametric sources of uncertainty (e.g., CESM CAM5/CAM6).

High-Performance Computing & Open Science

Experienced in processing and analyzing large climate datasets using CDO, NCO, and shell scripting in Linux/Unix environments, including execution on high-performance computing systems with optimized and scalable workflows. Maintains structured, version-controlled analysis pipelines to ensure reproducibility, transparency, and effective collaborative research.
